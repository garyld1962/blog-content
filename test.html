• Age: Age-related hearing loss is the most common cause of sensorineural hearing loss. The age at which symptoms start can provide important information.
  • Family history: A history of hearing loss or other related conditions can suggest a genetic cause.
  • Medications: Certain medications, such as aminoglycoside antibiotics or loop diuretics, can cause hearing loss as a side effect.
  • Noise exposure:Exposure to loud noises from occupational or recreational sources can cause hearing loss.
  • Illnesses and infections: Certain conditions or diseases, such as meningitis or mumps, can cause hearing loss.
  • Tinnitus: The presence of tinnitus (ringing or buzzing in the ears) can suggest inner ear damage.
